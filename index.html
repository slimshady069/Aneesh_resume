Frequently Asked Questions

What is HLLQP?

HLLQP (High Level Life and Qualifying Person) is a license that allows insurance brokers to sell life insurance products in Canada. It requires extensive training and passing rigorous exams to ensure brokers can provide expert advice on life insurance, critical illness, and disability insurance products.

Why choose an independent broker?

As an independent broker, I work with multiple insurance providers, giving you access to a wider range of products and better rates. I provide unbiased advice tailored to your specific needs, rather than being limited to a single company's products.

What types of insurance do you offer?

I specialize in life insurance, critical illness insurance, and disability insurance. This includes term life, whole life, universal life, and various riders to customize your coverage. I also offer investment and retirement planning solutions.

How do you determine my insurance needs?

I conduct a comprehensive needs analysis that considers your income, debts, family situation, and future goals. This helps determine the right type and amount of coverage to protect your financial future and provide peace of mind.

What is the process of getting insurance?

The process includes an initial consultation, needs analysis, product recommendations, application submission, and policy delivery. I guide you through each step and handle all the paperwork, making the process as smooth as possible.

How often should I review my insurance coverage?

It's recommended to review your insurance coverage annually or when you experience major life changes like marriage, having children, buying a home, or changing jobs. I provide regular policy reviews to ensure your coverage remains aligned with your needs.
